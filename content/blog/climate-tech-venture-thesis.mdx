## The Case for Climate Tech

The energy transition represents one of the largest investment opportunities of our generation. With trillions of dollars flowing into decarbonization, electrification, and grid modernization, the climate tech sector is attracting serious attention from venture and growth equity investors.

## Evaluation Framework

### Total Addressable Market

Understanding TAM in climate tech requires a nuanced approach. The markets are large but often fragmented, with different adoption curves across geographies and customer segments. Bottom-up TAM analysis—building from actual customer budgets and willingness to pay—is far more valuable than top-down estimates.

### Technology Risk vs. Market Risk

Unlike traditional software investments, many climate tech companies carry meaningful technology risk alongside market risk. The key question is whether the technology is proven at scale or still in the lab. The best investments are those where the technology works and the primary challenge is market adoption and go-to-market execution.

### Regulatory Tailwinds

Climate tech benefits from significant regulatory tailwinds—from the Inflation Reduction Act to state-level clean energy mandates. Understanding the regulatory landscape is critical to evaluating the durability of demand drivers.

### Unit Economics and Path to Profitability

Even with strong macro tailwinds, the fundamentals matter. Gross margins, customer acquisition costs, retention rates, and the path to cash flow profitability are just as important in climate tech as in any other sector.

## Conclusion

Building a thesis in climate tech requires patience, domain expertise, and a willingness to understand both the technology and the market dynamics. The opportunity set is enormous, but the winners will be the companies and investors who combine deep technical understanding with rigorous commercial analysis.
