## Introduction

The private equity landscape continues to evolve rapidly as we move through 2026. After years of elevated interest rates, shifting valuations, and a cautious exit environment, the industry is entering a new phase defined by selectivity, operational value creation, and sector specialization.

Having spent years on the other side of the table advising PE firms on commercial due diligence at McKinsey and EY-Parthenon, I want to share my perspective on where the market stands today.

## Key Trends Shaping the Market

### 1. Dry Powder and Deployment Discipline

Record levels of uninvested capital continue to sit on the sidelines. However, rather than rushing to deploy, the most disciplined firms are being highly selective about where they put capital to work. Valuation discipline has become a competitive advantage.

### 2. Operational Value Creation Takes Center Stage

The days of financial engineering driving the bulk of returns are behind us. Today, the best-performing funds are those with genuine operational capabilities—the ability to drive revenue growth, improve margins, and accelerate digital transformation within their portfolio companies.

### 3. Sector Specialization

Generalist funds are increasingly giving way to sector-focused strategies. Whether it is healthcare IT, climate technology, or vertical SaaS, the firms with deep domain expertise are winning deals and creating outsized value.

## Looking Ahead

As interest rates begin to normalize and the exit environment gradually improves, I expect we will see a pickup in deal activity through the second half of 2026. The firms that have invested in building genuine operational capabilities and sector expertise will be best positioned to capitalize on the opportunity ahead.

The private equity industry has always been cyclical, and the current environment—while challenging—is creating the conditions for the next generation of great investments.
